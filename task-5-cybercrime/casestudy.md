UPI Payment Fraud – Case Study

Online payment fraud through UPI is one of the fastest-growing cybercrimes in India. In a typical case, a college student receives a call from someone pretending to be a bank executive or a customer support agent. The fraudster claims that there is an issue with the student’s account or that they are eligible for a refund.

To resolve the issue, the scammer asks the student to scan a QR code or approve a “collect request” on their UPI app such as Google Pay or PhonePe. The student, believing it is a legitimate request to receive money, unknowingly authorizes a payment instead. In reality, money gets debited from their account.

Such scams often target students and young users because they actively use digital payment platforms but may not be fully aware of these tactics. The fraudsters use urgency and fear to manipulate the victim into acting quickly without thinking.

The consequences include immediate financial loss, stress, and sometimes leakage of sensitive banking information. Recovery of money is difficult if the fraud is not reported immediately. This case highlights the importance of awareness, verification, and cautious behaviour while using digital payment systems
